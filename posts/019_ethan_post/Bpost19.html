Introduction

In this post, I wanted to test a more practical use case for ChatGPT: career development. Specifically, I asked it to review my LinkedIn profile and resume—two things that students (myself included) constantly try to fine-tune but rarely get meaningful feedback on outside of the occasional career services walk-in. I wanted to see if ChatGPT could not only point out technical flaws but also make insightful recommendations that aligned with my goals in law, consulting, and public policy.

Linkedin

I provided ChatGPT with three screenshots of my entire linkedin and asked it for advice on how to improve it. It responded with the following:

“Your LinkedIn profile is already strong—it showcases excellent experience, leadership, and skills. Here are specific, high-impact suggestions to improve it even further:

1. Profile Banner and Headshot

  • Add a professional headshot: A clear, friendly, professional photo makes your profile far more trustworthy.
  • Add a background banner: Use something relevant (e.g. Penn campus, courtroom, or debate setting) to visually reinforce your identity.

2. Headline

Current: Philosophy, Political Science, and Economics @ University of Pennsylvania Suggested Update (more compelling): PPE Student at UPenn | Legal & Policy Intern | Teaching Assistant | Debate Coach | Passionate about Public Policy & Strategy*

This makes you more discoverable and instantly signals your value proposition.

3. “About” Section Improvements

Your “About” section is informative but could benefit from:

  • Stronger hook in the first line to capture interest
  • Bullet format to increase readability
  • Tie to future goals (e.g., law, policy, consulting)

Revised Version:

As a PPE student at the University of Pennsylvania, I thrive at the intersection of public policy, legal analysis, and strategic thinking.

🔹 Legal Experience: At Nelson Mullins, I contributed to case strategy and litigation prep through legal research and brief drafting. 🔹 Teaching & Leadership: As a Teaching Assistant for BEPP 2010 and Head Debate Coach at Germantown Friends, I lead discussions on public finance and empower students to become critical thinkers. 🔹 Results-Oriented Coach: Grew team performance 5x and led first-ever appearances at NSDA Nationals and TOC.

I’m driven by a desire to bridge rigorous analysis with real-world impact—whether in legal, consulting, or policy environments. Let’s connect!

4. Experience Descriptions

Your experience section is strong. A few tweaks:

Teaching Assistant - BEPP 2010

  • Add quantifiable metrics: “Led weekly sections of 20+ students,” or “Received 4.9/5 in student feedback (if applicable).”

Program Director - Germantown Friends

  • Consider clarifying: “Improved team performance by 5x” → e.g. “Boosted win rate from 10% to 50%”

Summer Legal Intern

  • Consider clarifying types of law or cases you worked on (e.g., civil litigation, insurance, etc.)
  • Add a line about tools used (e.g., LexisNexis, Westlaw)

5. Skills Section

  • You currently only show 2 skills. Add more relevant skills (7–10 total), such as:

    • Public Speaking
    • Legal Research
    • Strategic Planning
    • Policy Analysis
    • Team Leadership
    • Critical Thinking
    • Economic Policy
    • Teaching

Then ask 2–3 colleagues or mentors to endorse you for them.

6. Interests Section Add more companies and thought leaders (e.g., Brookings, McKinsey, Brennan Center, ACLU, DOJ, etc.) to show engagement and shape your feed.

7. Optional Additions

  • Licenses & Certifications (if you’ve done anything in legal research tools or data analysis)
  • Projects: Add your BEPP 2010 curriculum or debate team performance metrics as a project.
  • Volunteer work: If you’ve helped judge tournaments or done community work, include it!”

Analysis

ChatGPT’s LinkedIn feedback went beyond surface-level edits. It pinpointed clear gaps, missing visuals, a generic headline, and an understructured About section, and explained why they mattered to recruiters. More importantly, the advice was specific. It went section by section in my linkedin and provided specific ways I could improve each one. It also taught me about features that I never really thoguht about like the ability to upload projects and certifications. On top of that, it broke down the skills section and even explained endorsement.

Resume

I gave ChatGPT my resume and it returned the following:

“Your resume is already quite impressive — it reflects strong academic performance, leadership, and practical experience. Below are detailed suggestions to elevate it to a more competitive, professional level, especially if you’re applying for roles in consulting, policy, or law:

🔧 Formatting & Structural Edits

✅ General Improvements

  • Add a bold “Resume” title with your name (e.g., “Ethan Roytman — Resume”) at the top so it’s instantly recognizable.
  • Cut GPA from high school — keep your 5.75/4.0 GPA impressive, but it’s unnecessary once you’re in college.
  • Condense spacing: Too much white space between bullet points and sections wastes valuable room.

✏️ Content Feedback by Section

🎓 EDUCATION

University of Pennsylvania

  • Change to: University of Pennsylvania, Philadelphia, PA Bachelor of Arts in Philosophy, Politics, and Economics (PPE) Minors: Data Science & Analytics, American Public Policy GPA: 3.93/4.00 | Expected May 2027
  • Be consistent with title casing (capitalize majors/minors properly).

American Heritage

  • Remove GPA; just include name, location, and graduation date. It’s overshadowed by your Penn achievements.

💼 EXPERIENCE

Dynamic Debate Academy

  • Great quantification. Consider making the last bullet more outcome-driven: “Recruited and managed 4 coaches to sustain 100% client satisfaction as student demand quadrupled.”

Nelson Mullins

  • Strong descriptions. Slight polish:

    • “Conducted legal research on precedent and active cases to produce reports that guided litigation strategy.”
    • “Drafted briefs, motions, and contracts in compliance with legal and procedural standards.”

Germantown Friends School

  • Improve specificity in metrics:

    • “Improved team qualification rate by 5x…” → clarify baseline (e.g., from 2 to 10 qualifiers?)
    • Last bullet: “Created a 3-year growth plan encompassing recruitment, curriculum, and tournament strategy.”

🏫 LEADERSHIP & EXTRACURRICULARS

Teaching Assistant – BEPP 2010

  • Good content. You could make it more concise:

    • “Led weekly sessions and clarified complex topics in taxation, healthcare, and public spending.”
    • “Assisted grading and offered academic support to 40+ students.”

Wharton Undergraduate Energy Group

  • Excellent experience. Suggestions:

    • Begin bullets with stronger action verbs: “Developed market entry recommendations for Octopus Energy’s U.S. launch…”
    • Add result: Did your insights impact strategy? Improve adoption?

Speech and Debate Team

  • Terrific accomplishments. First bullet is strong.

    • Second bullet: rephrase to focus on outcomes: “Grew team 5x through targeted outreach, contributing to its rise to national top-10.”

🛠️ SKILLS

Expand and format more clearly:

Technical: Python, R, Excel, Tableau Legal: Legal research, brief writing, case strategy Professional: Strategic planning, public speaking, leadership, project management

If you know SQL, PowerPoint, Stata, etc., add them.

✨ Optional but High-Impact Additions

📌 Summary / Objective (top of resume)

Add a one-line professional summary to capture your profile quickly:

PPE student at Penn with a 3.93 GPA, strong legal research skills, and leadership experience in education and consulting.

📄 Projects (optional section)

If applicable, add 1–2 bullets under a “Projects” section, especially if you have done research, whitepapers, or case competitions (e.g., from Wharton classes).

✅ Final Checklist

✅ Criteria Done?
1-page only ✅
Bullet points start with action verbs ✅
No high school GPA ❌
Quantified achievements ✅
Skills are relevant & grouped ❌
File name is professional ⚠️ Rename to Ethan_Roytman_Resume.pdf

Analysis

Honestly, this is some of the best resume advice I have gotten from any source. It had a great mix of positive reinforcement and slight adjustments throughout my resume. It felt like ChatGPT had a purpose for every change/edit it suggested - it would explain how each slight change in wording could deliver a different message to recruiters. It also pointed out some things about my resume that I didn’t even consider. The most noticeable one was the fact that I don’t have it labeled professionally; I hadn’t even thought about a small detail like that, but it is definitely something that could affect how I am perceived.

Conclusion

ChatGPT proved to a very effective tool in career prep. It provided detailed feedback on both my Linkedin and Resume that was contextualized to its specific strengths and flaws. In the realm of Linkedin it taught my multiple new features, gave me ways to rephrase certain sections, and helped me create a headliner. In terms of resume development it picked up on tons of small details that helped me improved my resume and also balanced its criticism with positive reinforcement. Overall, I will definitely continue to use ChatGPT to help me improve my resume.
